## Executive Summary

Thailand is one of Southeast Asia's most active running markets, with hundreds of events annually and flagship races drawing 30,000–48,000+ participants. A local photo platform (**running.in.th**) already exists with face recognition search capability — making it a direct competitive signal. However, the broader photo delivery ecosystem is largely immature: most photos still flow through Facebook groups. The opportunity window for FMS is real but not uncontested.

## 1. Running Event Scene in Thailand

### Market Scale
Thailand has a highly developed running culture for SEA. Running began booming after the 1987 Royal Marathon (Rama IX Bridge), and growth has accelerated significantly through the 2010s–2020s.

**Key data points (confirmed):**
- **Amazing Thailand Marathon Bangkok (ATMBKK):** ~48,000 participants across all distances in 2025 (5,008 marathon finishers alone). Generates **THB 500 million (~USD 14M)** in Bangkok tourism annually. World Athletics Bronze Label. Organized by Thailand Tri-League for the Tourism Authority of Thailand (TAT).
- **Bangkok Marathon (BKK Marathon):** Established 1987, AIMS-certified, runs annually (next edition: November 15, 2026). Peak ~33,000+ all-distance participants (2015 data). BDMS-sponsored.
- Multiple regional marathons across Chiang Mai, Phuket, Khon Kaen, Pattaya, Hua Hin, etc.

### Estimated Race Count
- **⚠️ Assumption (not confirmed data):** Thailand likely hosts 200–400 running events per year across all distances and categories, based on regional comparisons (Malaysia ~300+/year, Philippines ~200+/year) and the confirmed scale of flagship events.
- Peak race season: October–February (cooler weather), with shoulder events year-round.

## 2. Race Photography Market

### Current Photo Delivery Methods

**⚠️ Partially confirmed, partially inferred from regional patterns + running.in.th existence:**

1. **Facebook Groups (dominant):** Most Thai race organizers and photographers distribute race photos via dedicated Facebook groups or pages. Runners search manually. This is the de facto standard for small-to-mid events.
2. **running.in.th (confirmed platform):** A Thai-built face recognition photo search platform. Title: "ค้นหารูปความประทับใจด้วยใบหน้า" = "Search for memorable photos by face." Live as of April 2026. Appears to be a SPA (single-page app), suggesting modern tech stack. This is the **primary domestic competitor**.
3. **Official event websites:** Large events (ATMBKK, BKK Marathon) likely host photos on their own sites or via contracted photographers. ATMBKK rules note: "Photos and videos are copyright of the organizing committee and may be used in media."
4. **Sportograf:** No confirmed evidence of significant Thailand presence. Sportograf is strongest in Europe and a few large Asian events. Not found in Thai context during research.
5. **USB / print:** Legacy delivery, declining. Some smaller events still offer printed photo packages at finish lines.

### Pricing Norms
- **⚠️ Assumption:** No confirmed pricing data found for Thai race photo sales. Regional benchmarks: Sportograf charges EUR 15–30/photo or EUR 20–50 for packages in Europe. For SEA markets, assume 150–500 THB per photo (USD 4–14) would be aspirational but unconfirmed.
- running.in.th's pricing model is **unknown** — the site is a React/Vue SPA that requires JavaScript rendering and couldn't be scraped. No public pricing page found.
- The majority of Thai race photos appear to be **free** (Facebook delivery), which creates a "free baseline" challenge for monetization.

### Gaps vs. FMS Offering
| Gap in Thailand | FMS Advantage |

| No dominant paid photo platform | FMS can own the paid photo market from the start |
| Facebook delivery = poor UX, no search | Bib number + face recognition search is massive UX upgrade |

| Organizers have no photo data/dashboard | FMS organizer dashboard is a new value-add |
| running.in.th lacks organizer integration (assumed) | FMS is an organizer-first platform |

### Willingness to Pay Signals
- **⚠️ Assumption based on market proxies:**
  - ATMBKK charges ~1,800–3,500 THB registration fees, suggesting runners have spend capacity.
  - Thailand's middle class + expat/tourist running community are price-acceptable for quality digital goods.
  - Face recognition novelty has high conversion potential (proven in PH market).
  - Runners who paid 2,000+ THB to race are likely to pay 150–400 THB for quality race photos.

### Key Entry Risks & Barriers
| Risk | Severity | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Language barrier | Medium | Thai UI/UX needed for mass adoption; English works for expat/tourist runners |
| "Free photos" cultural norm | High | Most Thai runners expect free Facebook photos; paid model needs justification |
| running.in.th incumbency | Medium | Already has face recognition, but unknown depth of organizer relationships |
| Payment methods | Medium | PromptPay (QR code) dominant in Thailand; credit cards common but cash-first culture; need PromptPay + TrueMoney + card integration |
| Local partner required | High | Direct sales to Thai organizers without a local BD or partner will be slow |
| TAT/government relationships | Medium | Major events are government-affiliated (TAT) — access requires trust/connections |
| Seasonal concentration | Low | Oct–Feb peak season means must be live before Oct to capture first full cycle |
| Legal/data privacy | Medium | Thailand PDPA (Personal Data Protection Act, 2022) governs biometric/facial recognition data — compliance required |

### Thailand PDPA Note
**⚠️ Important:** Thailand's Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) came into force June 2022. Face recognition/biometric data is classified as **sensitive personal data** requiring explicit consent. FMS must build a PDPA-compliant consent flow for Thailand.
