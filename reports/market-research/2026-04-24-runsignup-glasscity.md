### 2.2 RaceDay Photos — Feature Analysis
Based on RSU product documentation and feature menu:

**Features (confirmed):**
- Auto bib number tagging (optical bib recognition)
- Multiple photo albums per event
- Participant photo upload (crowdsourced photos)
- Watermarking tools
- Photo moderation by race director
- Photos integrated into results page (key engagement feature)
- Free to use for race directors

**Limitations (confirmed gap vs FMS):**
- ❌ No face recognition / AI face search
- ❌ No participant self-search by face
- ❌ No photo commerce engine (pricing, packages, bundles)
- ❌ No highlights reel or video product
- ❌ Requires participants to know their bib number
- ❌ Basic photo display, not a monetization platform

**Pricing:** Free for race directors. Unclear if photo sales are enabled (likely not — RSU's model is free tools, no upsell on photos).

**Verdict:** RaceDay Photos is a photo-hosting/discovery convenience feature, not a revenue-generating photo product. FMS is building a photo commerce engine with AI — these are not the same product.

### 2.3 RunSignUp's Competitive Position (US Registration Market)
**Main competitors to RSU:**
- **ACTIVE Network / RunnerSpace** — historically #2, declining. Parent company instability.
- **RaceRoster** — Canadian, acquired by D-League (now IRONMAN-aligned). Growing in triathlon/running.
- **Cvent / Race Roster** — corporate events, less endurance focus.
- **Haku** — newer, premium race management. Small market share.
- **Chronotrack** — recently shut down its registration product (RSU gained those customers).
- **Stack/GetMeRegistered/RaceWire** — recently closed. RSU absorbed customers.
- **Eventbrite** — general events, weak in endurance, expensive.

**RSU's moat:** Free, purpose-built, deep endurance feature set, 60% market share, US-only focus. Competitors are either shutting down or going upmarket/niche.

### 2.4 US Race Photography Market
*(Numbers are estimates unless noted)*

**Market size:**
- ~30,000–40,000 endurance events/year in the US
- Average race: 500–5,000 participants
- Photo purchase rate: ~10–25% of finishers (industry estimate)
- Average photo package price: $20–$60
- **Estimated TAM: $150–400M/year** (rough estimate based on participation × conversion × ASP)

**Main photo vendors:**
| Vendor | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| **MarathonFoto** | Legacy giant. Covers majors (Boston, NYC, Chicago). Bib-based search. Premium pricing. No face recognition. Declining share at mid-tier events. |
| **FinisherPix** | Acquired by Outside (media company, owns Outside+, Gaia GPS, MapMyFitness). Now integrated into Outside+ ecosystem. Bib + face recognition in some deployments. Growing. |
| **RaceDay Photos (RSU)** | Free, basic, no face search. RSU's captive photo solution for its 30,000 events. |
| **BibRave / BibTag** | Photo tagging service, smaller. |
| **SportPhoto** | European origin, expanding US. AI face recognition. FMS's closest direct competitor in tech approach. |
| **Local/Regional Photographers** | Majority of mid-size race market. No tech platform. Price-sensitive. |

**Key gap:** The mid-tier US race (500–5,000 participants) is largely unserved by a modern AI face-recognition photo commerce platform. MarathonFoto dominates majors. Locals dominate small events. The 2,000–10,000 participant range is the prize.
